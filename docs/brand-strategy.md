# TIZIRI — Brand Strategy

## Name & Meaning

**TIZIRI** — from Tamazight (Berber), meaning *moonlight*.

Chosen to replace the original working name MAISON ATLAS, which was retired after discovering a domain conflict with an existing brand (maison-atlas.com). TIZIRI is short, distinct, pronounceable in French and English, and carries a poetic Berber resonance that connects directly to the Atlas Mountain origin of the rugs.

---

## Positioning

**Category:** Luxury handwoven Moroccan rugs — direct-to-consumer  
**Audience:** Design-conscious buyers in Europe, North America, and Australia seeking authentic, singular pieces — not catalogue product  
**Price tier:** Premium (pricing TBD per rug)  
**Distribution:** Online-first, worldwide shipping

**Core differentiation:**
- Every rug is one of a kind — never replicated, never restocked
- Sourced directly from Berber artisans in the Atlas region (not through intermediaries)
- Editorial presentation — the site functions more like a gallery than a shop

---

## Brand Voice

Restrained. Confident. Literary without being pretentious. Short declarative sentences. No superlatives. No hard selling.

**Principles:**
- Say less, mean more
- Trust the object — let the rug speak
- Never use the word "luxury" — demonstrate it instead
- Avoid generic e-commerce language ("add to cart", "shop now") in favour of quieter phrasing

**Tone examples (from the live site):**
- *"Every rug is singular."*
- *"Woven in Morocco. Made for one home."*
- *"We bring fewer pieces than most. That is the point."*
- *"When it finds its home, it is finished."*
- *"No noise. Unsubscribe any time."*
