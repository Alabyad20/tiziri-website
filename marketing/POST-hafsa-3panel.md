## Why it's written this way

Built on what the account data actually showed (see `INSTAGRAM-caption-fix.md`):

- **Opens with a reason to stop scrolling, not the rug's name.** The first ~125 characters are all most people see; your five worst-performing posts spent that space on a repeated SEO title.
- **Names the format explicitly** ("three views… top panel… bottom is the wool up close"). Split-screen is unusual in this niche — pointing at it earns the extra second of attention that drives watch time.
- **Leads on the colour anomaly.** "Normally just ivory and black" gives a reason this rug is unusual, which is more shareable than "handwoven in Morocco."
- **Ends on a question, not a CTA.** You have **zero comments across 19 posts**, and Instagram weights comments far above likes. A cheap either/or question ("bedroom or living room") is the lowest-friction way to break that duck.
- **Nine hashtags, mixed.** Broad (`#MoroccanRug`) for reach, narrow (`#DopamineDecor`, `#MaximalistDecor`) because that's where a 4-follower account can actually rank.

The video already carries the price and the "save this one" ask on screen, so the caption doesn't repeat them — it does the job the on-screen text can't.
