**Be honest about this number:** 18 posts, best result 3 likes, **zero comments ever**. At that
volume the caption comparison above is suggestive, not proof. The account's main problem is that
almost nobody is seeing the posts yet — captions alone will not fix that. What the caption fix
*does* do is stop wasting the only line most people ever read.

**The concrete, non-debatable defect:** Instagram truncates the caption preview at roughly
125 characters. That title line is 62–68 characters of pure boilerplate, and it is *repeated*
by the next line which starts with the rug's name again. So on those 5 posts, the entire visible
caption is a duplicate SEO title. Nobody taps "more" on a line that already looks like a filename.

## Fix 3 — the caption formula from here on

Every caption, four parts, in this order:

1. **A first line under 100 characters that is not the rug's name.** This is the only line most
   people read. Make it a claim, a contradiction, or a question — "Nobody expects a Beni Ourain
   in lime." Never open with `Name — Handwoven Moroccan Wool Rug`; that is a *title*, not a caption.
2. **Two or three sentences of the specific thing** — the colour that shouldn't work, the
   free-hand line, the carved pile. Specific beats poetic.
3. **Size + one trust fact** (in stock, ships from the US, one of one) + the URL.
4. **6–10 hashtags**, mixing broad (`#MoroccanRug`) with narrow (`#DopamineDecor`, `#ArtForTheFloor`).
   Narrow tags are where a 0-follower account can actually rank.

**Ask for a comment.** Zero comments across 18 posts is the loudest signal in the data — Instagram
weighs comments far above likes. End one post a week with a real question: *"Lime or ivory — which
one's going in your room?"*

The captions already sitting in `BUFFER-posting-queue.md` follow this shape correctly. Paste
those verbatim rather than retyping a title line in Buffer — that's where the boilerplate crept in.
