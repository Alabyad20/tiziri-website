Gift Guide July 2026

Moroccan Rug Gift Guide

Most gifts are used up, worn out, or forgotten within a year. A genuine hand-knotted rug is one of the few gifts that's designed to outlast the occasion it marks by decades — which is exactly what makes it a meaningful choice for the milestones people actually remember. Here's how to choose one for someone else's home with confidence.

Housewarming

A compact rug — small enough to work in an entryway, a reading nook, or layered over a larger piece already in the home — is the safest housewarming size, since you're rarely certain of someone's exact room layout ahead of time. A Boujaad or Boucherouite in a compact format brings genuine character without requiring the recipient to build a whole room around it.

Wedding & registry

A rug is an unusual but genuinely practical registry addition — unlike most registry items, it's something a couple will use daily for years, and a larger living-room-sized piece (see our living room guide for sizing) makes a substantial, memorable gift that smaller items on a typical registry can't match. If buying without knowing the couple's décor plans, an ivory Beni Ourain is the safest choice — its neutral palette works in almost any home they eventually furnish.

Anniversary

For a milestone anniversary, a vintage piece carries a genuine parallel to the occasion itself — a rug that has already lasted decades, given as a marker of years already shared. This is one gift occasion where the vintage vs. new choice has real symbolic weight, not just a practical one.

Quick picks by occasion
  • Housewarming: compact Boujaad or Boucherouite, under 200cm
  • Wedding / registry: ivory Beni Ourain, living-room sized
  • Anniversary: a vintage piece with real age and history
  • Uncertain of their space: a runner or compact piece — versatile in almost any room

"Most gifts are chosen to be liked immediately. A good rug is chosen to still be there in twenty years — that's a different, and arguably more meaningful, bar to clear."

If you're not sure what to choose

When in doubt, size down and neutralise the palette — a smaller, ivory or warm-neutral piece is far more likely to suit a home you haven't seen than a large, boldly colored statement rug. Contact us directly with the occasion and any details you know about the recipient's home, and we're glad to help narrow it down.

The bottom line

A hand-knotted rug rewards the same instinct as any genuinely good gift: it says the giver thought about permanence, not just the moment of opening it. Choose by occasion first, then size and color to reduce the risk of a poor fit in a home you may not know as well as your own.
